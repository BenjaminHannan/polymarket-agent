# Polyagent: External Fixes for Ten Identified Failure Modes (2024–2026 Literature)

## TL;DR
- The dominant, most well-supported finding across the 2024–2026 Polymarket microstructure literature (Bartlett & O'Hara 2026, Akey/Gregoire/Harvie/Martineau 2026, Della Vedova 2026) is that **edge on Polymarket comes from execution (maker-side liquidity provision), not from prediction accuracy** — directly contradicting the current Polyagent design, where the model is taker-only with a calibration-first philosophy. The single highest-leverage change is therefore to land the queue-aware fill simulator and convert the system to maker-default execution before any further alpha work.
- Pure forecasting alpha is small and shrinking: Mantic+Tinker (Mar 2026), Turtel et al. 2025 (DPO self-play, arXiv 2502.05253), and Outcome-RL (arXiv 2505.17989) demonstrate that small (8–14B) RL/DPO-finetuned forecasters can reach frontier Brier on judgmental questions, but ForecastBench Oct 2025 still shows the *best* RAG-LLM (GPT-4.5, Brier 0.101) only marginally beats market consensus and trails superforecasters by 0.02 — Akey 2026's "shrink toward market" prior is the only consistently positive recipe.
- Residual inefficiency on Polymarket is concentrated in (a) NegRisk rebalancing and combinatorial arb (Saguillo et al. AFT 2025: $40M extracted, $29M from NegRisk), (b) sub-second mispricings on niche/illiquid books (Yang/Cheng/Zou 2026 NBA: median 3.6s episode), and (c) insider/local-edge wallet detection (Dubach 2026, Sirolly et al. Nov 2025) — all of which are maker-friendly, not taker-friendly.

## Key Findings

### Problem 1 — Adverse selection on taker side
Confirmed by *three* independent 2026 papers (Bartlett & O'Hara SSRN 6615739; Akey et al. SSRN 6443103; Della Vedova SSRN 6191618). Akey et al. explicitly find **a 1-σ increase in maker volume share lowers loss probability by 9.3 percentage points**, and Della Vedova (222M trades) shows retail picks winners 51.3% but loses $79M while bots earn $133M with coin-flip accuracy — the entire delta is execution.

**Concrete fixes (signal:effort ranked):**
1. **Switch to post-only / GTC maker default** with cancel-on-toxicity guard. Reference implementations: `nkaz001/hftbacktest` (queue-position fill model with latency); `warproxxx/poly-maker` (Polymarket-native maker bot using bands); `Polymarket/poly-market-maker` (official keeper, AMM + bands strategies); `elielieli909/polymarket-marketmaking` (multi-band reference). Signal: very high (the literature agrees). Effort: medium — already most of what queue-aware sim unlocks.
2. **Avellaneda–Stoikov reservation-price quoting with VPIN/OFI toxicity skew.** Modern implementations: Barzykin–Bergault–Guéant–Lemmel (arXiv 2508.20225, "Optimal Quoting under Adverse Selection and Price Reading," 2025) extends A–S with explicit informed-flow term and price-reading correction; Barzykin (arXiv 2603.07752, Mar 2026) adds slippage-control rejection (FX context, transferable). Open-source: `fedecaccia/avellaneda-stoikov`; `market_maker_rs` crate (Rust). Signal: high. Effort: medium.
3. **Cont–Kukanov–Stoikov OFI as queue-aware alpha rather than as taker signal.** The Cont/Kukanov/Stoikov 2014 framework (SSRN 1712822) gives a linear-in-OFI price-impact model; Moallemi (2016, queue-value paper) is the canonical extension covering the option-value of queue position. For 2-second Polygon block latency the practical adaptation is to use OFI to *withdraw* maker quotes, not to take.
4. **Cancel-latency drift / Brownian σ√Δt cancel model** rather than instantaneous cancellation, plus rejection feedback (Olding 2022, formalised in Barzykin 2026) — directly addresses the Polymarket-specific "ghost fills" issue that V2 only partially closed (Polymarket Nov 2025). Open-source: hftbacktest's `power_prob_queue_model`.
5. **Adverse-selection-aware sizing via VPIN gating.** Bartlett & O'Hara 2026 explicitly validates VPIN on Kalshi prediction markets — single-name markets have predictable VPIN→maker-loss correlation; broad markets do not. Practical: gate maker quotes by 5-min one-sided OFI threshold per category.

### Problem 2 — Model +0.14 log-loss worse than market
ForecastBench Oct 2025 leaderboard puts top LLM (GPT-4.5) at Brier 0.101 vs superforecaster 0.081 vs market questions where the crowd is the benchmark; the gap to *liquid* markets remains positive for almost all small open models. ForecastBench arXiv 2409.19839 found Claude-3.5 at Brier 0.119 on resolved questions, still 0.05 worse than humans. New 2025–2026 evidence:

**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Akey 2026 "shrink toward market" prior** (your existing log-pool combiner with ≥60% market weight is exactly the published recipe — keep it; the published paper validates that this single design choice is the difference between profit and loss for non-bot wallets). Increase market weight further on liquid books and below-confidence-threshold cells; relax only when (a) confidence ≥0.7 *and* (b) cell has ≥80 calibration samples.
2. **Outcome-driven DPO self-play (Turtel et al. 2025, arXiv 2502.05253):** Phi-4 14B / DeepSeek-R1 14B gain 7–10% Brier from self-play DPO with no human labels, reaching GPT-4o parity. Implementation cost on a single RTX 5070 Ti is feasible with 4-bit/NVFP4 (the Blackwell paper arXiv 2601.09527 confirms Qwen3-14B fits with 16k context).
3. **Outcome-RL / ReMax (arXiv 2505.17989, May 2025):** scaled to 110k synthetic forecasting questions, 14B model matches o1 on Brier (0.193) and beats it on ECE (0.042 vs higher). Reports a $127 vs $92 hypothetical-trading-profit p=0.037 — the only paper with measured trading edge from RL fine-tuning.
4. **Mantic + Tinker (Thinking Machines, March 2026):** RL fine-tuning of gpt-oss-120b on 10k Aug 2024–Dec 2025 questions lifts Metaculus Brier-index from 38.6 to 45.8, on par with frontier general models. Confirms the recipe but at a parameter scale beyond a 5070 Ti.
5. **Market-Conditioned Prompting (arXiv 2602.21229, "Forecasting Future Language: Context Design for Mention Markets"):** explicitly treat market-implied probability as a Bayesian *prior* and condition LLM update on textual context — a published, formal version of the log-pool combiner. Useful for prompt redesign.
6. **Consistency-rewarded training (Paleka et al. 2024 arXiv 2412.18544; "Scaling Open-Ended Reasoning to Predict the Future" arXiv 2512.25070):** train forecasters with arbitrage-based consistency loss; correlates strongly with held-out Brier.
7. **LLM ensemble / wisdom-of-the-silicon-crowd (Schoenegger et al. 2024, *Science Advances*):** 12-LLM median is statistically indistinguishable from a 925-human crowd. Higher-order aggregation (arXiv 2510.01499, "Beyond Majority Voting") improves further when accuracy/correlation are estimated.

### Problem 3 — Kyle's λ collapse
Tsang & Yang 2026 (SSRN 6336679, arXiv 2603.03136) shows λ falling from ~0.53 to ~0.01 on the Trump market. Where alpha lives now:

**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **NegRisk arbitrage and combinatorial constraints** — see Problem 6. Saguillo et al. AFT 2025 documents $40M extracted (Apr 2024–Apr 2025), $29M from NegRisk rebalancing; top wallet $2.01M across 4,049 trades.
2. **Sub-category targeting:** Yang/Cheng/Zou 2026 (SSRN 6624718) on Polymarket NBA finds in-game arb opportunities median 3.6s and concentrated in final minutes; Dubach 2026 (arXiv 2604.24366) confirms longshot spread premium (a documented stylized fact). Direct your model to longshot, late-resolution, and category-illiquid sub-pools where residual edge persists.
3. **Local-edge / insider-wallet copy-following.** Della Vedova's wallet-orthogonality test flags 6,292 wallets out of 483K with too-high accuracy (p<0.01) — concentrated in Action and Vote markets. Open-source implementations: `pselamy/polymarket-insider-tracker`, `al1enjesus/polyclawster`, `Drakkar-Software/OctoBot-Prediction-Market` (copy-trading). Polysights' "insider information finder" beta is the commercial example. Polymarket+Chainalysis (Apr 2026) is now actively suppressing these wallets, so the edge is rapidly closing — but copy-trading on confirmed-skill bot wallets is supported by Akey 2026's profit-concentration finding (top 1% capture 76.5%).
4. **Trade-direction inference from on-chain `OrderFilled` events, not from public WebSocket feed.** Dubach 2026's striking finding: feed-based Lee-Ready agrees with on-chain ground truth only ~59% of the time vs 80%+ on equity venues, and Kyle's λ flips sign on 60% of markets between feeds. Any microstructure feature using public feed direction is largely noise.
5. **Foreign-language / weather / niche markets:** the Anatomy of Polymarket paper does not isolate λ by category, but the documented narrowing happened on highly-traded election contracts; 2025–2026 Polymarket added crypto-15min, sports, and weather books which have not been studied at the same depth — these are plausible λ-residual venues.

### Problem 4 — Paper broker optimism (overstates P&L 20–40%)
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Adopt `nkaz001/hftbacktest`** (3.3k★, Rust+Python) as backbone for the queue-aware sim. Already implements: queue-position fill, configurable feed/order latency, `power_prob_queue_model`, partial fills, L2/L3 reconstruction. Updated Feb 2025 with explicit `power_prob_queue_model=3` (more pessimistic queue) for the post-2024 regime.
2. **Use `agent-next/polymarket-paper-trader`** (MCP-server, Polymarket-native): level-by-level book walking, exact bps×min(p, 1−p)×shares fee model, GTC/GTD state machine, slippage tracked in bps vs midpoint.
3. **Polygon-specific cancel-latency model:** sub-50 ms median archive-ingestion delay with multi-second tail (Dubach 2026 stylized fact #6). Encode as Brownian σ√Δt drift on cancel-arrival time with heavy upper tail, and explicitly simulate "ghost fills" (off-chain fill, on-chain settlement failure) that V2 only partially eliminated. AWS eu-west-2 (London) hosting + ~73 ms baseline server processing means the *floor* on round-trip is much higher than typical exchange backtests assume.
4. **Olding 2022 / Barzykin 2026 last-look + slippage-tolerance model:** symmetric cancellation when mid moves beyond threshold during latency window, formalised — the conservative way to model Polymarket cancel-replace.
5. **VWAP fill is too generous;** prefer level-walking + queue position + (latency × σ) drift on top-of-book. Validate by comparing Jan–Feb 2026 backtest vs your live ~190 fills as the hftbacktest authors recommend.

### Problem 5 — Statistical significance bottleneck (~190 fills)
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Bayesian Sharpe with t-likelihood and weakly informative priors** (Kruschke BEST adaptation; pyfolio's Bayesian tearsheet; `stefan-jansen/machine-learning-for-trading` notebook 03). At n=190, a Bayesian posterior over μ/σ gives meaningful credible intervals where the frequentist Sharpe gate is uninformative. Mulligan QMF 2024 ("Bayesian Estimation for Sharpe Ratios under Selection Bias") is the recent extension with Bayesian counterpart to DSR.
2. **Hierarchical / partial-pooling model across (category × horizon) cells** (brms / Stan random-effect intercepts per cell). Borrows strength across cells; especially valuable for sports_global which is your only certified cell. CRAN rstanarm `pooling.html` is a canonical reference. The simulation in Springer's IRT paper (s13428-022-02000-5) shows partial-pooling unbiased at n=500 per cell across configs — at n≈190 *aggregate*, hierarchical is essentially mandatory.
3. **CPCV remains best at this scale** — Arian/Norouzi/Seco (SSRN 4686376; ScienceDirect 2024) confirm CPCV beats walk-forward, K-fold and purged K-fold on PBO and DSR test statistic. Open-source: `mrbcuda/pbo` (R), and quantbeckman has working CPCV code. With 23+ gate combinations, run CSCV/CPCV explicitly and report PBO alongside DSR=0.996.
4. **Stationary block bootstrap for trade-level Sharpe CI** under autocorrelation — pair with Ledoit–Wolf 2008 robust SR test as multiple-test backstop.
5. **Bayesian regime-switching robust Sharpe (artifact-research.com)** — quantile-of-Sharpe-distribution rather than point estimate; particularly applicable when only sports_global is certified.

### Problem 6 — NegRisk / combinatorial constraints
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Saguillo et al. AFT 2025 (arXiv 2508.03474, IMDEA):** the canonical 2025 paper. Methodology: Linq-Embed-Mistral embeddings + LLM relationship extraction → reduces O(2^(n+m)) comparison to tractable. Released code path on IMDEA dspace. Documents 7,051 single-condition arb-opportunity conditions and 662 NegRisk-rebalancing markets. Implement their reduction heuristic before searching.
2. **Yang/Cheng/Zou 2026 NBA (SSRN 6624718):** real-time order-book-snapshot detection rather than executed-trade reconstruction; finds median 3.6 s episode — sets the realistic latency bar. They explicitly discuss capital-cap from min-leg-liquidity, which is your sizing constraint.
3. **Karkare/Paleka consistency check (arXiv 2412.18544):** turn the consistency metric into a *training* loss (used in Outcome-RL and Scaling Open-Ended Reasoning to Predict the Future); your current implementation is detector-only.
4. **AMM-aware NegRisk detector:** Polymarket's actual NegRisk mint/burn mechanism (CTF + position split) means a sum-to-1 violation is only an arb if minimum-leg liquidity supports it net-of-fees — the Yang/Cheng/Zou methodology incorporates this. Cross-check `Polymarket/agent-skills` `ctf-operations.md` for the on-chain primitives.
5. **Beyond NegRisk: monotonicity arbs and event-id grouping.** Saguillo Section 5 enumerates 13 dependent U.S. election pairs in 2024 with 5 yielding profit; these were monotonicity (e.g., "Trump wins" ≤ "Republican wins") rather than NegRisk — a separate constraint family worth detecting. Cross-platform Kalshi↔Polymarket spreads (Ng et al. SSRN 5331995, 2025) yield further inter-platform arb but with oracle-divergence risk (Mar 2025 UMA governance attack: 25% voter manipulated $7M market).

### Problem 7 — Calibration on thin cells
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Manokhin Probability Matrix (arXiv 2605.03816, "The Manokhin Probability Matrix," 2025):** key practical finding for you — **LightGBM is a "Bull" (strong AUC, poor calibration), and Venn-Abers cuts log-loss 6.5–12.6% on Bulls** but *degrades* well-calibrated models. Validates current ≥30 Venn-Abers tier; reinforces decision rule "don't apply post-hoc calibration to already-calibrated cells."
2. **Generalized Venn-Abers (arXiv 2502.05676, "Generalized Venn and Venn-Abers Calibration," ICLR 2025) + `ip200/venn-abers`:** Vovk/Petej-style set calibrators with finite-sample guarantees and a quantile-loss extension. Replaces your isotonic ≥80 with set-valued Venn calibration that gives an epistemic-uncertainty interval — directly usable for Kelly fractional sizing.
3. **Hierarchical Bayesian calibration across (category × horizon)** — see Problem 5 fix #2; same machinery, applied to calibration parameters. brms hierarchical Beta calibration with hyperprior over category-level shape parameters is the most direct upgrade to your current ≥15 Beta tier.
4. **Conformal-Kelly sizing (Vovk 2019/2020 conformal predictive distributions, alrw.net Working Papers 23–25; Vovk May 2025 "Conformal e-prediction" arXiv 2001.05989).** Use conformal predictive distribution as the input to distributionally-robust Kelly (Sun & Boyd, "Distributional Robust Kelly Gambling," arXiv 1812.10371) — robust to your already-flagged "Kelly Betting Can Be Too Conservative" failure mode (arXiv 1710.01786).
5. **Conformal Risk Control (ICLR 2024, OpenReview f3549ef9b5ff5)** for choosing per-cell abstention thresholds with finite-sample guarantees.

### Problem 8 — Confidence-threshold selective abstention
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Heng & Soh ICLR 2025 ("Know When to Abstain: Optimal Selective Classification with Likelihood Ratios," arXiv 2505.15008)** — proves the Neyman-Pearson optimal selection score is a likelihood ratio; provides RLog and Δ-KNN-RLog as practical scores; explicitly handles covariate shift (which your live-vs-train regime experiences). Drop-in replacement for current confidence-threshold rule.
2. **"Trading via selective classification" (Coletta et al., ACM ICAIF 2021; later extensions surveyed in ACM Computing Surveys 2025 doi:10.1145/3727633):** ternary classification (UP / FLAT / DOWN) lets the model abstain on small-magnitude moves; directly supports your monotonic-Sharpe-with-confidence finding.
3. **Geifman & El-Yaniv risk-coverage curves** with Conformal Risk Control to set thresholds with finite-sample guarantees; generalises the current "≥0.7 confidence" hard cut to a coverage-controlled abstention.
4. **Asymmetric scoring rules / cost-sensitive Brier:** weight loss by inverse coverage so the model is rewarded for covering more high-confidence cells.

### Problem 9 — RAG / LLM forecaster
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Train, don't just retrieve.** Outcome-RL (arXiv 2505.17989) and Turtel DPO (arXiv 2502.05253) both achievable on RTX 5070 Ti (16 GB) with 8B–14B models and NVFP4/W4A16 (arXiv 2601.09527 confirms Qwen3-8B-NVFP4 viable with 16k context, sub-second TTFT). **This is the highest-signal RAG/LLM upgrade.**
2. **Replace Phi-4-mini with Qwen3-8B-NVFP4 or Qwen3.5 small series** (Unsloth Qwen3.5 docs, Mar 2026 update). Sebastian Raschka's State-of-LLMs-2025 survey identifies these as the current efficient-tier sweet spot. gpt-oss-20B is the next step up if VRAM allows.
3. **ColBERT modernization:** ModernColBERT / GTE-ModernColBERT-v1, ColBERT-Att (arXiv 2603.25248), and PyLate (arXiv Aug 2025) are 2025–2026 late-interaction upgrades with rotary embeddings and pruning. ColPali/ColQwen if you need to ingest screenshots/images (e.g., congressional PDFs). For your RSS+SEC+CourtListener corpus, ModernBERT+ColBERTv2 two-stage (Rivera et al., MDPI Electronics 2026 doi:10.3390/electronics15030541) is the documented current best.
4. **Multi-LLM probability ensemble** (Schoenegger et al. 2024 *Science Advances*; arXiv 2510.01499 higher-order aggregation). Run Qwen3-8B + gpt-oss-20B + a third decorrelated model; aggregate via accuracy-weighted (not equal-weight) median.
5. **Forecasting-specific benchmarks beyond ForecastBench:** Metaculus AI Benchmark Tournament (Mantic-style), arXiv 2507.04562 ("Evaluating LLMs on Real-World Forecasting Against Expert Forecasters," Brier on resolved political/economic questions); arXiv 2602.21229 mention-markets benchmark.

### Problem 10 — Alternative data / feature edges
**Concrete fixes (ranked):**
1. **Wallet-level orthogonality test (Della Vedova 2026 dellavedova.com / SSRN 6191618):** flags 6,292 informed wallets out of 483K with realized accuracy too high to be luck (p<0.01); concentrated in Action and Vote markets, lowest in Stochastic. Implementable as a per-wallet feature joining your existing on-chain ingest. Della Vedova publishes a weekly index update.
2. **Sirolly et al. (Nov 2025, SSRN 5714122) network-based wash-trade detector.** Iterative graph clustering to identify approximately closed counterparty subgraphs. Use as a *negative* feature (suppress signal in markets with high wash-share) rather than signal: the paper documents wash-share peaks at 60% Dec 2024, 20% Oct 2025; sports the worst-affected category. Combine with Polymarket's Apr 2026 Chainalysis self-policing.
3. **Polymarket maker-rewards as alpha amplifier:** $12M distributed in 2025; quadratic-spread scoring formula (docs.polymarket.com/market-makers/liquidity-rewards). Wanguolin's Medium "two-week deep dive" frames it as bonus-not-engine but documents survival-analysis approach to fill modeling.
4. **OFI on multi-level book** (Cont-Kukanov-Stoikov 2010/2014; arXiv 2602.00776 Dec 2025 cross-asset SHAP-stable patterns: order-book-imbalance and adverse-selection features rank stably across BTC/LTC/ETC/ENJ/ROSE). For thin Polymarket books, micro-price and VAMP improve over mid; queue-imbalance is a one-tick-ahead predictor (Gould & Bonart 2015).
5. **Cross-platform divergence (Kalshi vs Polymarket; Ng et al. 2025 SSRN 5331995):** documented spread that has compressed but not closed; oracle-risk-bounded (15-cent buffer recommended after the Mar 2025 UMA event).
6. **Adjacent News / Adjacent / market-data terminals (Adjacent News, Marketlens, Dome — `aarora4/Awesome-Prediction-Market-Tools`)** — documented commercial feeds explicitly built for prediction-market alt-data ingestion.

Replaces both the thin-cell calibration fallback chain *and* the n=190 statistical-significance bottleneck in one model. Lets you certify additional categories via posterior credible-interval rather than waiting for 1,500 trades. ## Caveats

- **Date-specific claims:** Several SSRN/arXiv papers are dated 2026 (Bartlett & O'Hara Apr 2026; Della Vedova Feb 2026; Akey et al. Mar 2026; Tsang & Yang 2026; Dubach Apr 2026; Yang/Cheng/Zou Apr 2026; Mantic+Tinker March 2026). I have reported their findings as documented but a single-author project should independently verify the headline numbers (e.g., 61%/32% YES-resolve gap from Bartlett & O'Hara; $29M/$40M NegRisk arbitrage from Saguillo).
- **The Polymarket microstructure regime is shifting fast:** Polymarket V2 launched late 2025 fixing some "ghost fills"; Chainalysis insider-detection went live Apr 2026; ICE invested up to $2B Oct 2025; Kalshi is now CFTC-licensed and Polymarket US re-launched Nov 2025 via QCX. Edges measured pre-Apr 2025 (the IMDEA window) may have compressed by 30–50% by 2026 — `navnoorbawa.substack.com` writeups explicitly project this. Treat all published $-extraction figures as *upper bounds* on what is now extractable.
- **Wash-trading inflates volume metrics** (Sirolly et al. Nov 2025: ~25% average, peaks 60%). Any feature using *volume* (not trade count or net flow) is contaminated; sports volume is the worst-affected.
- **Trade-direction inference from public WebSocket feed is ~59% accurate vs ~80% on equities** (Dubach 2026). Any current OFI/Lee-Ready feature in Polyagent needs to migrate to on-chain `OrderFilled` events.
- **takakhoo/Polymarket_Agent**: I was unable to confirm this specific repo exists at that path; the closest live references are `Polymarket/agents` (official), `0xPhysis/Polyagent` (this project's namesake), `al1enjesus/polyclawster`, and `pselamy/polymarket-insider-tracker`. The "local-edge classifier" pattern referred to is best instantiated by Della Vedova's wallet-orthogonality test or Polysights' beta insider-finder, not a single canonical repo.
- **Mantic/Tinker results** are reported by Mantic (a commercial vendor) on a guest post at Thinking Machines Lab, not in a peer-reviewed venue, and use Metaculus baseline-score rather than Brier as the headline metric. Treat the "frontier-model parity at 8B" claim as preliminary.
- **Forecasting alpha vs market-implied probability remains marginal even at frontier scale** (ForecastBench Oct 2025: best LLM Brier 0.101, market crowd ~0.11 on liquid; gap is small and may flip on illiquid/new questions). Akey 2026's blunt finding that profit comes from execution rather than information should temper expectations on any forecaster-side improvement.
- **DSR=0.996 with n=190 and 23 gate combinations** itself warrants a CSCV/PBO sanity check before relying on it; Bailey-Borwein-Lopez de Prado-Zhu 2014 (SSRN 2326253) explicitly warns DSR alone undercorrects when many gate variants are tried.
